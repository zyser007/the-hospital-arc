Everything in this story is told entirely from my point of view.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PROLOGUE

I had been drinking heavily for 3-4 days in a row because I had several social gatherings to attend.

During that time, I was already experiencing stomach pain from my existing stomach problems—which was hardly surprising, considering the perfectly healthy and well-balanced lifestyle of a gamer and programmer.

— I’m joking… but only halfway. LOL. —

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

10 JULY — 09:00

I woke up, had breakfast, and vomited blood.

That was the moment I realized this was no longer something I could simply ignore and sleep off.

— This is bad. I really need to see a doctor. —

STATUS EFFECT: ANXIETY — MAX

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

10 JULY — 12:00

I arrived at the hospital and entered the familiar realm of registration, queues, and preliminary examinations.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

10 JULY — 14:30

The doctor told me that I would need to stay in the hospital overnight for observation and undergo an endoscopy.

That meant I was no longer allowed to eat or drink anything.

The doctor did not specify exactly when the procedure would take place.

And I did not have the courage to ask.

— It’ll probably be around ten tomorrow morning… I guess. —

Then began the process of finding me a hospital room.

By that point, I had already completed my transformation into Kamen Rider V3.

Tubes and wires stretched from my body in every direction.

My left hand was connected to medication that reduced stomach acid, while my right hand was attached to an IV drip.

Both were connected to two devices that looked like ancient Nintendo consoles from our grandparents’ generation.

These machines had the incredibly cool name:

BODYGUARD

Unfortunately, these two Bodyguards became a major problem for me.

I am afraid of needles.

Having the IV attached made me feel as though both of my hands had been locked in place. I was afraid to move them too much because I thought it would hurt.

The nurse had already explained that the part left inside my vein was made of plastic, not metal.

I understood the explanation perfectly well.

— I know it’s plastic… but I’m still scared anyway. —

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

10 JULY — 18:00

I had spent several hours lying around uselessly in an OPD room.

What happened inside that room shall remain unspoken.

Eventually, the room-booking process was completed.

It had taken an incredibly long time.

— This is a private hospital, and I’ve already paid part of the medical expenses in advance. —

But another part of me tried to be reasonable.

— Maybe there are other patients whose conditions are more urgent and important than mine… or perhaps it’s simply an internal coordination problem. —

Once I finally reached my room, I began replaying the entire day in my head.

— Should I have been allowed to eat something before the fasting period began? —

— Shouldn’t someone have explained the schedule or at least told me what was supposed to happen next? —

Instead, I had simply been left there alone, confused, and waiting without any clear purpose.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

11 JULY — 09:00

I spent the entire night with stomach pain, hunger, and broken sleep.

Sometimes I fell asleep. Sometimes I suddenly woke up, only to find the clock staring back at me.

My mind was filled with food.

Menus.

Dishes.

Anything edible.

— Chicken rice… noodles… basil stir-fry… anything. Just give me something. —

By morning, a nurse came to wake me up.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

11 JULY — 10:00

The doctor came to ask about my symptoms and told me that I would soon be taken for the endoscopy.

— I’m hungry. —

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

11 JULY — 11:00

An ambulance transported me to another building—the one used for surgeries and childbirth.

I was placed in a preparation room with several other patients.

My bed was the first one brought into the room.

After that, more patients gradually arrived one by one.

I lay there waiting.

And waiting.

And waiting.

— I’m starving. When is this ordeal finally going to end? —

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

11 JULY — 12:00

At last, I was moved into the examination room, and the endoscopy procedure began.

— Yes! Finally! It’s actually happening! —

Everything inside the room was white, and it was extremely cold.

The doctor sprayed a local anesthetic into my mouth, positioned me on my side, placed a protective mouthpiece between my teeth, and injected a sedative through the IV line in my right hand.

A cold sensation spread through my hand.

Then my consciousness disappeared.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

11 JULY — 12:15

I woke up on a bed outside the examination room.

I assumed it was a shared recovery area.

I remembered the room number. I remembered where I was positioned. I could see the clock, and I could hear the nurses nearby chatting and laughing together.

What happened inside this room shall also remain unspoken.

I pretended not to care.

— Whatever. I’ll probably be allowed to eat soon. —

But I was wrong.

I was awake.

I was conscious.

I moved my body.

I looked directly at the people around me.

Time continued to pass, yet no one came to speak to me.

— Couldn’t someone at least say, “The doctor will come and explain your results shortly”? —

Nothing.

Absolutely nothing.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

11 JULY — 12:50

The doctor finally came to my bedside and explained the examination results.

My condition was not very serious.

I could either go home immediately or remain in the hospital for further observation.

— I don’t want to spend more money, I’m hungry, and I hate staying in hospitals. Of course I’m going home. —

So I chose to leave.

After that, I continued lying there, starving and without purpose.

— When is one of the nurses finally going to call my name? —

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

11 JULY — 13:30

At last, my turn had come.

A nurse called my name.

— Finally! Victory is mine! —

I was transported back to the original building by ambulance while still lying on the hospital bed.

Then I was taken all the way back to my room on that same bed.

Under normal circumstances, I probably should have been transferred into a wheelchair. I was fully conscious and perfectly capable of sitting up.

— Honestly, I could probably stand up and dance right now. Just take me somewhere I can eat. Hooray! —

But there was one problem:

The cursed BODYGUARD machines.

The staff pulled at them, adjusted them, and tried to figure out which device belonged where.

“This one belongs to this ward.”

“That one belongs to the other ward.”

“This model is larger.”

“That model is smaller.”

They swapped one machine for another, then swapped them back again.

Eventually, no one could find a clean solution.

So they decided to transport the entire bed and sort everything out afterward.

I was not upset about this part.

— Fair enough. It’s a good solution to an immediate problem, and I don’t have to keep getting up and sitting down. —

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

11 JULY — 15:50

I arrived home.

CUT TO BLACK.

Just like the ending of a low-budget movie.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

EPILOGUE

After returning home, I sat down and reflected on everything that had happened.

From my perspective, these were the lessons I learned:

• I now fully understand the meaning of the saying, “Good health is the greatest blessing.”

• I am not young anymore. I really should take better care of myself.

• When buying insurance, pay attention to the details. Do not buy several policies and then forget which one covers what.

• Anything left unfinished or unresolved in life should be dealt with properly.

• If the Hunger status remains active for too long, it eventually evolves into Gluttony.

• The male nurse likes SangSom.

• The OPD nurse likes green tea and mala tang.

• The doctor was both beautiful and kind.

• At this point, absolutely everything tastes delicious.

BANKAI!!
